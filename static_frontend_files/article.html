Is Jalen Brunson's success a product of his team, or is he a true winner?

By Arav Arora

May 3rd, 2024

Jalen Brunson has steadily emerged as one of the prominent guards in the NBA, showcasing his scoring ability, resilience, and leadership on the court. His journey from a promising rookie to a key player is a testament to his dedication and hard work. Below is a chart illustrating his points per game (PPG) from 2018 to 2023:

2018: Brunson entered the NBA in the 2018-2019 season with the Dallas Mavericks. In his rookie year, he averaged 8.8 points per game. Adjusting to the pace and physicality of the league, he showed glimpses of his potential. His ability to navigate the complexities of the NBA as a rookie was impressive, and he quickly became a fan favorite for his tenacity and court vision.

2019: In his sophomore season, Brunson improved slightly, averaging 9.3 points per game. While the increase was modest, it was clear that he was gaining confidence and understanding of the game. His improved decision-making and shooting accuracy made him a more reliable option for the Mavericks. Brunson's ability to handle pressure situations started to shine through, providing a glimpse into his future potential.

2020: The 2020-2021 season saw a more significant jump in Brunson's scoring. He averaged 12.6 points per game, establishing himself as a reliable scorer off the bench for the Mavericks. His ability to drive to the basket and finish in traffic became more apparent. Brunson also improved his three-point shooting, making him a versatile offensive threat. His contributions were crucial in several close games, earning him praise from coaches and analysts alike.

2021: Continuing his upward trajectory, Brunson averaged 16.3 points per game in the 2021-2022 season. His role expanded, and he became a crucial part of the Mavericks' rotation, often stepping up in critical moments. Brunson's leadership on and off the court was becoming evident, as he often served as a calming presence during high-pressure situations. His work ethic and determination were key factors in his continued improvement.
